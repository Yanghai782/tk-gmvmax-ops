# A1-A5 Audience Funnel

## The Five Stages

| Stage | Name | Definition |
|-------|------|------------|
| A1 | Aware | First-time exposure to your creative |
| A2 | Interested | Engaged with creative (watch time, clicks) |
| A3 | Considering | Added to cart / browsed store but didn't buy |
| A4 | Converted | Completed a purchase |
| A5 | Loyal | Repeat purchaser |

## How Cold Start Uses the Funnel

The system's job during cold start: learn which users move from A1 -> A4.

- System starts with broad A1 exposure
- Creative quality determines A1 -> A2 filtration rate
- Product page + offer determines A2 -> A3 progression
- Price + trust determines A3 -> A4 conversion
- CPA stabilizes as the system builds a reliable A1->A4 model

## Creative Strategy by Funnel Stage

### A1 -> A2 (Awareness to Interest)
- Hook-driven creative: first 2 seconds critical
- Broad appeal, problem-solution framing
- Goal: maximize 2-second and 6-second play rates

### A2 -> A3 (Interest to Consideration)
- Product details, usage scenarios, social proof
- Goal: maximize click-through and store visits

### A3 -> A4 (Consideration to Purchase)
- Price anchoring, urgency, guarantees
- Goal: maximize conversion rate from product page

## The Funnel Math

`
Conversion at each stage is multiplicative:

A1 (impressions) 
  * 2s play rate = A2 entry
  * CTR = A3 entry  
  * CVR = A4

A drop at any stage kills overall efficiency.
`

## Key Insight

Cold start = system learns the A1->A4 probability for your product.
The faster it sees A4 conversions, the faster CPA drops.
This is why creative quality (A1->A2 rate) and product-market fit (A3->A4 rate) 
determine cold start success.

Creative wins at A1->A2. Product wins at A3->A4.
Both must work for GMV Max to deliver.
